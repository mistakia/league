# Genesis League Rules

Genesis League is a 10-team dynasty, superflex fantasy football league with a $200 salary cap, dynasty contracts, extensions, franchise tags, restricted free agency, and a practice squad with poaching. This page is a summary; the [full Genesis League Constitution](/constitution) is the authoritative source and includes every article, definition, and the amendment history.

## Format

- 10 teams, weekly head-to-head, half-PPR scoring with 6-point touchdowns.
- Regular season, then the Wildcard round (Week 15), then a two-week Championship (Weeks 16-17).
- Divisions are redrawn every year.

## Roster (16 players)

- Starters: 1 QB, 2 RB, 2 WR, 1 TE, 1 Superflex (QB/RB/WR/TE), 1 Flex (RB/WR/TE), 1 D/ST.
- 7 bench spots, 3 Injured Reserve slots, and unlimited Long-Term IR.

## Salary cap and contracts

- $200 salary cap, enforced during the Offseason and on Opening Day.
- Each Offseason a team may retain any set of players whose combined values fit under the cap. Extending a player raises its value, escalating by $5 for each extension already used.
- One rookie per year may be extended for $0 (rookie tag).
- One franchise tag per year, valued at the average of the top contracts at the player's position; a player cannot be franchise tagged three years in a row.

## Free agency

- Offseason free agent auction with a $200 budget.
- In-season acquisitions run through a blind-bid FAAB waiver system ($200 budget). Waiver order follows the draft order and drops to the bottom after each successful claim.
- Restricted Free Agency: a team may nominate up to two of its own players; the original team keeps the player unless another team's blind offer beats it by more than 20% or $2 (whichever is greater).

## Practice squad and poaching

- Up to four signed free agents plus unlimited rookie-draft players.
- Another team may poach a practice-squad player for $2 above their contract; the player goes straight to the poaching team's active roster.
- When a poached player is released, the original team gets super-priority to reclaim them. Sanctuary periods restrict when poaching claims can be submitted.

## Rookie draft

- Three rounds, straight order.
- Non-playoff draft order is set by a Draft Order Index: 90% Potential Points (best-possible-lineup scoring) and 10% All-Play Losses, lowest index picks first.
- Rookie contracts run from $12 for the first pick down to a $1 minimum.

## Playoffs and payouts

- Wildcard (Week 15): four teams, the two highest scorers advance.
- Championship (Weeks 16-17): four teams over two weeks, highest combined score wins.
- Payouts: 62% of the pot to the champion, 10% to the runner-up, and 2% to the highest single regular-season weekly score.

## Governance

- The Commissioner administers the league, approves transactions, and resolves disputes. Decisions are reviewable: one-third of eligible teams can petition, and a two-thirds vote can overturn.
- Amendments require a two-thirds Offseason vote (unanimous during the Regular Season).
- Offences such as collusion, bribery, and non-competitive behaviour carry penalties up to permanent suspension.
